## Overview

A three-degree-of-freedom manipulator built around a parallelogram mechanism, designed for pick-and-place tasks. The parallelogram linkage keeps the end-effector orientation constant throughout the workspace, simplifying the kinematic model.

## Kinematics

Both forward and inverse kinematics were derived fully analytically — no numerical methods. The closed-form solution allows real-time trajectory planning with guaranteed solutions within the workspace.

$$
\mathbf{p} = f(\theta_1, \theta_2, \theta_3)
$$

The inverse kinematics maps a desired end-effector position back to joint angles, handling the multiple solution branches inherent to the parallelogram geometry.

## Implementation

- Mechanical design and fabrication (additive manufacturing + laser cutting)
- Electronics: stepper motors, controller boards
- Control: trajectory interpolation and execution in C++
